Udaipur – The City of Lakes

Udaipur, often called the “Venice of the East,” is famed for its shimmering lakes, marble palaces and romantic ambience. Founded by Maharana Udai Singh II in 1559, it is the historic capital of Mewar.

The Mewar region has been inhabited since early times, with traces of Chalcolithic and later tribal settlements. Udaipur was established in 1559 CE by Udai Singh II after the fall of Chittorgarh. It grew as the capital of the Sisodia Rajputs, resisting Mughal influence and later entering alliances with the Marathas and British. Today it is celebrated for its palaces, lakes, gardens and rich cultural traditions.

  • c. 2000–1000 BCE: Chalcolithic sites and tribal habitation in Mewar plains.
  • 6th–13th c.: Rise of Sisodia Rajputs; stronghold at Chittorgarh.
  • 1559 CE: Maharana Udai Singh II establishes Udaipur after losing Chittorgarh to Mughals.
  • 16th–18th c.: Mewar capital flourishes with palaces, gardens, temples; often in conflict yet maintaining independence from Mughals.
  • 19th c.: Treaty with British ensures princely status; modernization begins.
  • 1947–present: Integration into Rajasthan; Udaipur becomes a global tourist destination and cultural hub.

Top Attractions

City Palace – Iconic palace complex on Lake Pichola ➕
Built over centuries, City Palace is a magnificent blend of Rajasthani and Mughal architecture. It houses museums, courtyards, balconies and offers panoramic views of the lake.
Lake Pichola – Boat rides & island palaces ➕
Created in 1362, Lake Pichola is surrounded by palaces and ghats. A boat ride offers stunning views of Jag Mandir and the famous Lake Palace.
Sajjangarh (Monsoon Palace) – Hilltop fort with sunset views ➕
Perched on a hill, the 19th-century Sajjangarh Palace overlooks Fateh Sagar and the city. It is famed for sunset vistas and as a monsoon retreat.
Jag Mandir – Island palace on Lake Pichola ➕
Built by Maharanas of Mewar, Jag Mandir is a serene island retreat with marble chhatris, gardens and stunning lake surroundings.
Fateh Sagar Lake – Scenic lake with boat rides & parks ➕
Fateh Sagar, with its three islands, offers boating, Nehru Park and views of surrounding hills—a popular leisure spot for visitors and locals.
Best Time: Sep–Mar
Nearest Airport: Maharana Pratap Airport (22 km)
Railway: Udaipur City Station
Stay: 2–3 days

Suggested Itinerary

1 Day: City Palace → Lake Pichola boat ride → Jag Mandir.

2 Days: Day 1 as above, Day 2 Sajjangarh, Fateh Sagar, local markets.
