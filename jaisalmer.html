Jaisalmer – The Golden City

Home Jaipur Jodhpur Jaisalmer Udaipur Pushkar Kota Mount Abu Chittorgarh

Jaisalmer, known as the Golden City, is famous for its sandstone architecture, desert landscapes, and historic havelis. It is located in the heart of the Thar Desert.

Established in 1156 CE by Rawal Jaisal, Jaisalmer developed as a trading hub in the Thar Desert. Its massive fort, intricately carved havelis, and proximity to desert dunes attract travelers worldwide.

  • 12th c.: Jaisalmer Fort constructed by Rawal Jaisal.
  • 15th–18th c.: Patwon Ki Haveli and other mansions built.
  • Modern era: Tourism, desert safaris, and cultural heritage popularize Jaisalmer globally.

Top Attractions

Jaisalmer Fort – Massive desert fort ➕
A UNESCO World Heritage site, it dominates the city skyline and houses temples, shops, and residences inside.
Patwon Ki Haveli – Lavish sandstone mansion ➕
Famous for ornate carvings and architecture, representing the wealth of Jaisalmer’s merchant families.
Gadisar Lake – Historic water reservoir ➕
Built in the 14th century, it provides a serene spot for boating and sunset photography.
Sam Sand Dunes – Desert safari & cultural shows ➕
Experience camel rides, folk performances, and stunning sunsets over the Thar Desert dunes.
Best Time: Oct–Feb
Nearest Airport: Jodhpur Airport (285 km)
Railway: Jaisalmer Railway Station
Stay: 2–3 days

Suggested Itinerary

1 Day: Jaisalmer Fort → Patwon Ki Haveli → Gadisar Lake.

2 Days: Day 1 as above, Day 2 Sam Sand Dunes desert safari and cultural activities.
