Jaipur – The Pink City

Jaipur, the Pink City, is Rajasthan’s capital and a UNESCO World Heritage city. Known for its royal palaces, forts, and vibrant bazaars, Jaipur represents the soul of Rajputana architecture.

Founded in 1727 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II, Jaipur was India’s first planned city. Its pink-colored buildings symbolize hospitality. The city is famous for gems, handicrafts, and its grand heritage structures.

  • 18th c.: City founded by Jai Singh II, construction of City Palace and Jantar Mantar.
  • 19th c.: Pink makeover for Prince Albert’s visit.
  • Modern era: UNESCO heritage city, hub of culture and tourism.

Top Attractions

Amber Fort – Majestic hill fort ➕
Built in red sandstone and marble, Amber Fort showcases Rajput architecture with ornate halls and Sheesh Mahal.
Hawa Mahal – Palace of Winds ➕
Iconic 5-storey palace with honeycomb windows, built for royal women to view street life unseen.
Jantar Mantar – Astronomical observatory ➕
UNESCO site with world’s largest stone sundial, built by Jai Singh II for precise astronomical calculations.
City Palace – Royal residence ➕
A blend of Rajput and Mughal styles, still partly the residence of Jaipur’s royal family.
Best Time: Oct–Mar
Nearest Airport: Jaipur International Airport
Railway: Jaipur Junction
Stay: 2–3 days

Suggested Itinerary

2 Days: Amber Fort → Hawa Mahal → Jantar Mantar → City Palace → Markets (Johari, Bapu).

How to Reach

Air: Jaipur Airport, well connected to Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata.
Rail: Jaipur Junction links major Indian cities.
Road: Delhi–Jaipur highway (NH48), buses and taxis widely available.
