Chittorgarh – Historic Fort City

Home Jaipur Jodhpur Jaisalmer Udaipur Pushkar Kota Mount Abu Chittorgarh

Chittorgarh, the historic capital of Mewar, is famed for its massive fort, Rajput valor, and medieval architecture. It is one of the largest forts in India.

The city has been a symbol of Rajput bravery and culture. Chittorgarh Fort has witnessed sieges, heroic battles, and tales of Rajput queens. Its towers, palaces, and temples reflect the grandeur of Mewar rulers.

  • 7th–12th c.: Establishment and expansion of the fort.
  • 13th–16th c.: Vijay Stambh and Kirti Stambh constructed; historic battles.
  • Modern era: Tourism, heritage walks, and cultural festivals.

Top Attractions

Chittorgarh Fort – Massive historic fort ➕
Spanning 700 acres, it includes palaces, gates, temples, and towers. UNESCO World Heritage site.
Vijay Stambh – Tower of Victory ➕
Built by Rana Kumbha in 1448 to commemorate victory over Muslim rulers, adorned with intricate carvings.
Kirti Stambh – Tower of Fame ➕
Dedicated to Jain Tirthankaras, it is a 22-meter tall tower with detailed sculptures.
Padmini Palace – Historic palace of Rani Padmini ➕
Known for its Rajputana architecture and legendary tales of Queen Padmini.
Best Time: Oct–Mar
