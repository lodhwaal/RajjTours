Pushkar, a sacred town in Rajasthan, is famous for its holy lake, temples, and the vibrant Pushkar Camel Fair. The town attracts pilgrims, tourists, and photographers alike.

Pushkar has been a prominent pilgrimage site for centuries, known for its Brahma Temple, sacred lake, and religious fairs. The town combines spirituality with vibrant Rajasthan culture.

  • c. 10th–12th c.: Pushkar develops as a holy town with temples and ghats.
  • 17th–18th c.: Construction of Brahma Temple and ghats around Pushkar Lake.
  • Modern era: Pushkar Camel Fair and tourism bring international attention.

Top Attractions

Pushkar Lake – Sacred lake and ghats ➕
Pushkar Lake is considered holy; pilgrims take ritual baths at its ghats and temples surround its banks.
Brahma Temple – Only temple dedicated to Lord Brahma ➕
Built centuries ago, this temple attracts pilgrims and visitors from around the world. Its architecture is distinctive with a red spire and intricate carvings.
Savitri Temple – Hilltop temple with panoramic views ➕
Located on a hill, Savitri Temple provides panoramic views of Pushkar town and lake; reachable via 900+ steps.
Camel Fair Grounds – Annual Pushkar Camel Fair ➕
The grounds host the famous Pushkar Camel Fair each year with trading, competitions, cultural shows, and vibrant festivities.
Best Time: Oct–Nov
Nearest Airport: Jaipur International Airport (145 km)
Railway: Ajmer Junction
Stay: 1–2 days

Suggested Itinerary

1 Day: Pushkar Lake → Brahma Temple → Savitri Temple.
