Kota – The City of Lakes & Forts

Kota, located on the banks of the Chambal River, is known for its forts, gardens, and scenic riverside views. It is a historic city with a rich cultural and architectural heritage.

Kota has been inhabited since ancient times, with historical structures from the Bundi and Kota rulers. The city grew around the Chambal River and became known for its forts, palaces, and public gardens.

  • c. 10th–12th c.: Bundi-Kota region under local rulers, construction of forts.
  • 17th–18th c.: Kota developed as a princely state with palaces, gardens and riverfronts.
  • 19th c.: Kota Fort, Kishor Sagar and City Park established and maintained.
  • 1947–present: Integration into Rajasthan; city known for education, tourism, and riverfront attractions.

Top Attractions

River Front Kota – Scenic riverside promenade ➕
The riverfront area of Kota along the Chambal River offers walking paths, gardens, and serene views of the city and river.
Kishor Sagar Talab – Historical lake with palace views ➕
Kishor Sagar Talab is a picturesque lake in Kota featuring palaces and gardens on its banks, ideal for photography and leisure walks.
Kota Fort – Historic fort overlooking the city ➕
Kota Fort, built by local rulers, features massive walls, gates, and panoramic city views. A symbol of Kota's royal past.
City Park – Public park and leisure area ➕
A popular spot for families and tourists, City Park offers gardens, walking trails, and open spaces for relaxation.
Best Time: Oct–Mar
Nearest Airport: Kota Airport (8 km)
Railway: Kota Junction

Suggested Itinerary

1 Day: River Front Kota → Kishor Sagar Talab → Kota Fort.
